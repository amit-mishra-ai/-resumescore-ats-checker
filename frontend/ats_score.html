Chapter 1

What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by 99% of Fortune 500 companies and over 75% of all employers to filter, rank, and manage job applications before a human ever reads them.

When you apply online, your resume is parsed by the ATS into structured fields — name, email, skills, job titles, education. Then it's scored against the job description. If your score is too low, you're automatically rejected — even if you're perfectly qualified.

Understanding this system is the single highest-leverage thing you can do to improve your interview rate.

Chapter 4

The 10 ATS Commandments

Follow these rules and your resume will pass through every major ATS system:

01
Use a clean single-column layout
Multi-column layouts confuse ATS parsers. Stick to one column for experience, education, and skills. Tables are read left-to-right and often jumble content.
02
Use standard section headers
Name sections exactly: "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Certifications". Creative names like "My Journey" or "What I've Done" will not be recognized.
03
Mirror the job description keywords
Paste the job description into our checker to see which keywords you're missing. If the JD says "React.js" don't just write "React" — use the exact phrasing when possible.
04
Quantify every achievement you can
"Improved performance" is weak. "Reduced API response time by 38% serving 50k daily users" is ATS gold. Numbers boost both ATS scores and human recruiter interest.
05
Save as .docx or standard PDF
Use Word's built-in PDF export or Google Docs. Never use Canva, Photoshop, or design tools to create your resume PDF — they embed text as images.
06
Never put info only in headers/footers
Page headers and footers are invisible to most ATS parsers. Put your name, email, phone, and LinkedIn in the main body of the document, not the document header.
07
Avoid images, logos, and graphics
No profile photos, company logos, skill graphs, or decorative icons. ATS systems skip images entirely. Skill bars (e.g. ████░░ Python) are meaningless to parsers.
08
Don't use uncommon fonts or unicode
Stick to Arial, Calibri, Garamond, or Times New Roman. Decorative fonts and unicode symbols (✦ ► ★) can cause parsing errors on older ATS platforms.
09
Include dates on every role
Every job and degree needs start and end dates (month/year preferred). ATS uses dates to calculate tenure and rank recency. Missing dates can cause miscalculation of your experience level.
10
Tailor for every application
One resume fits no one. Spend 10 minutes per application adjusting your summary and skills to match the job description. Our checker makes this fast with JD keyword gap analysis.

Chapter 6

Frequently Asked Questions

Does ATS score affect my chances even if a human reviews my resume? ▾
Yes. ATS systems rank and sort candidates. Recruiters typically start from the top of the ranked list. Even if every application is technically "reviewed", low-ranked resumes often get only a 2-second glance. A high ATS score gets you seen first.
Should I use a one-page or two-page resume? ▾
ATS has no preference for page count — it reads text, not pages. For humans: under 5 years experience = 1 page, 5–10 years = 1–2 pages, 10+ years = 2 pages max. Never pad to fill space. Quality over quantity.
Can I use a Canva or design-template resume? ▾
Only if you're applying in person or to a company that doesn't use an ATS (rare). For any online application, design-tool resumes are extremely risky. Canva PDFs embed text as images that ATS cannot read. Your content becomes invisible.
How many keywords should I include? ▾
Quality beats quantity. 15–25 relevant, naturally placed keywords is ideal. Keyword stuffing (listing "Python Python Python" repeatedly) is detected and penalized by modern ATS. Use keywords contextually in your bullet points, not just a keyword dump in the skills section.
Does LinkedIn "Easy Apply" go through an ATS? ▾
Yes. LinkedIn Easy Apply forwards your application to the employer's ATS. Your LinkedIn profile data or uploaded resume is parsed the same way. ATS optimization applies equally to Easy Apply submissions.
What's the best resume format for freshers / new graduates? ▾
Lead with Education, then Projects, then Skills, then any internships or part-time work. Include relevant coursework, academic achievements, and certifications. Our ATS checker automatically detects fresher profiles and adjusts scoring — experience section is not penalized.
